Deadlocked

by: Thomas Snyder

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because I didn't want to make a platformer or any sort of top down game. Additionally, I liked the idea of a rogue-like that didn't require a large amount of strategy but still had enough to provide a challenge

2. What I Learned

I learned about many bugs and errors I did not expect to occur normally. I also obtained advice on changes to better the quality of the life for players like providing visible statistics and adding a feature to the merchant where you can buy multiple items in one usage.

3. Architecture of My Game

The game has a map and combat which allow for the player to maneuver and strategize optimal pathing and optimal combat.The Deadlocked class provides the combat and setup for the UI, the Encounters provides the resources and randomization of tiles, and the Player class defines player movement.

4. Technical Implementation

The game is quite simple, a 9x5 board where you are one square and you can only move to adjacent squares. The end goal is to beat the dragon, but to do that you must defeat the goblins, but to do that you need items, but to get items you need to find them or buy them, but to buy them requires gold, but to get materials and gold requires you to move, and movement on tiles other than goblin tiles brings you closer to an ambush.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part was the bug fixing. I spent many weeks (and hours as home) working on fixing many bugs and causing new ones every time. It became numbingly boring to fix error after error after error, and never actually seeing progress in the game's development. I overcame this, by pushing through the mundane problems and then implementing new ideas as a sort of reward.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

In the future I'd like to streamline the trader's shop using JButtons as entering text to enter different segments of the shop and then entering text again to buy an item, and then entering text to leave the shop is repetitive and wastes time.
